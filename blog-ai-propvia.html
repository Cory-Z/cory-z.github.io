Designing AI-assisted property insights at Propvia

How I worked as a Mobile Developer Intern on an AI-powered property analytics product and what I learned about making complex data feel simple and human.

By Corien Zavod · 2025

Starting with a messy, real-world problem

When I joined Propvia as a Mobile Developer Intern, the goal wasn’t just to ship another app screen — it was to help investors make faster, smarter decisions about real properties in real neighborhoods. The product pulls in a lot of data: vacancy information, risk indicators, neighborhood metrics, and more. Without good UX, all of that can quickly turn into noise.

My job was to help bring Propvia’s web experience to mobile while keeping it focused, approachable, and useful in the moments when investors actually need it — on the go, in the field, or between meetings.

Where AI comes in

Under the hood, Propvia uses AI to evaluate properties and surface insights that would be hard to see at a glance. Instead of asking users to dig through raw numbers, the product can highlight patterns, risks, and opportunities and turn them into plain-language guidance.

For me as a UX-focused developer, that meant thinking about questions like:

  • How do we present AI-generated insights so they feel trustworthy, not mysterious?
  • How much detail should we show by default vs. behind a tap?
  • How can we let users drill into the data without overwhelming them?

Designing the mobile experience

On mobile, screen space is tight, attention is short, and context is constantly shifting. I collaborated with designers to adapt the design system to mobile, then implemented key flows using React Native and Expo. We focused on:

  • Making property summaries scannable in a few seconds
  • Highlighting the most important AI-driven callouts first
  • Keeping actions like saving, comparing, or sharing properties easy to reach

Throughout the internship, I iterated based on feedback from the team, adjusting layouts, copy, and interactions to make sure the AI never felt like a black box bolted onto the side of the app. Instead, it needed to feel like a natural extension of the product.

What I learned about AI-assisted product design

The biggest lesson for me was that “AI features” aren’t that interesting on their own. What matters is how they show up in the experience. Some takeaways:

  • AI should reduce cognitive load, not increase it.
  • Clear, honest language builds more trust than technical jargon.
  • Users appreciate having both a quick summary and a way to dig deeper.

Working on Propvia showed me how UX, engineering, and AI can come together to solve real problems and not just as a buzzword, but as part of a thoughtful, human-centered product.

Going forward, I’m excited to keep exploring how AI can support people’s decision-making without taking control away from them. Whether it's in real estate, event navigation, or whatever problem I get to work on next, I'm look forward to seeing where AI takes us.
