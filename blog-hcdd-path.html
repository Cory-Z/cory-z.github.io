How I found my way into Human-Centered Design

I didn’t start out planning to be a UX or product designer. I started in engineering, and eventually realized that what I cared about most wasn’t just how systems worked, it was how people experienced them.

By Corien Zavod · 2025

Starting in engineering

My first step into tech was through engineering. I liked solving problems, building things, and understanding how systems fit together. But as I progressed, I noticed that the parts of projects that excited me most weren’t the calculations or the specs, but instead they were the moments where I could think about the person on the other side of the screen.

I found myself asking questions like: Who is this for? Will this actually make their day easier? What does this look and feel like when someone is tired, stressed, or in a hurry? Those questions didn’t always fit neatly into a traditional engineering mindset, but they always stuck with me.

Discovering Human-Centered Design

That’s what eventually led me to Human-Centered Design & Development at Penn State. HCDD felt like the missing piece. It felt like a place where caring about interfaces, flows, and user emotions wasn’t a side habit, it was the entire point.

I learned how to connect research, design, and development into a single process: Talking to users, mapping their journeys, sketching out solutions, prototyping, and then actually building them. It was engineering, but with people fully in the center of every decision.

Why digital experiences clicked for me

The more I worked on interfaces and digital products, the more I realized how much impact they can have. A small change in a flow can save someone time every single day. A clear screen can reduce anxiety. A smarter layout can make complicated information feel manageable.

I loved the idea that I could design something online that quietly improves someone’s life. Whether it helped them navigate a busy campus, understand a property investment, or just find what they need without thinking too hard, I became invested.

Why HCDD feels like the right path

Human-Centered Design & Development gives me a way to bring together everything I care about: the structure and logic of engineering, the creativity of design, and the empathy of talking to real people. It’s a path where I get to build things that work and feel right.

Today, when I look at the projects in my portfolio, from event navigation to AI-powered property tools, I see the same thread running through all of them: a focus on real users, real problems, and experiences that try to respect people’s time and attention.

That’s the kind of work I want to keep doing as I move into full-time roles: designing and building digital experiences that are not just functional, but genuinely helpful.
